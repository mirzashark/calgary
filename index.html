Why Proper Insulation Matters

Upgrading your wall insulation is an investment in your home’s long-term health and efficiency:

  • Lower Utility Bills: Reduces heating and cooling costs year-round.
  • Improved Efficiency: Creates a high-performance thermal barrier.
  • Enhanced Comfort: Eliminates drafts and ensures temperature control.
  • Air-Sealing: Prevents pollutants and moisture from entering.

When to Replace Insulation

Over time, insulation degrades. Knowing when to upgrade is key to home efficiency:

  • Age of Insulation: Homes over 15-20 years lose R-value and efficiency.
  • Drafts & Swings: Cold spots or temperature struggles indicate gap settling.
  • Visible Contamination: Rodent activity, mold, or decay require cleanout.
  • High Energy Bills: Workload spikes on HVAC point to insulation leaks.
  • Post-Renovation: Upgrades seal newly modified spaces cleanly.
  • Expert Rule: Professional checks every few years spot hidden bugs.

Moisture & Weather Impact

Leaks or rain cause insulation to fail and trigger structural hazards:

  • Loss of R-Value: Wet insulation drops insulating power instantly.
  • Mold & Mildew: Wall cavities become breeding grounds for bacteria.
  • Wood Rot: Wood structural frames decay under trapped dampness.
  • Pest Influx: Moisture draws insects and rodents into wall spaces.
  • Odors: Trapped decay smells cycle through vents into rooms.
  • Inspection Note: Leaks require urgent wall cavities checks to protect health.
